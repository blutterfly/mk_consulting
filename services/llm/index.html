Below is a “menu” of LLM-powered services you can pitch to a healthcare client that holds \~4 million electronic medical records (EMRs). I’ve grouped them by outcome and spelled out the data, privacy, and deployment considerations you’ll need to cover in every statement of work (SOW).

1. Clinical Documentation & Summarization¶

What you deliver

Capability Typical Outputs Impact
Real-time note summarizer SOAP or APSO progress-note drafts, referral letters Cuts charting time \~50 % and physician after-hours work
Discharge-summary generator Problem-list reconciliation, medication changes, follow-up plan Faster bed turnover, fewer hand-off errors
Patient-friendly visit recap 5th-grade-level summary + action items Boosts comprehension & CAHPS scores

How – Fine-tune a domain model such as MedLM or GPT-Med-5 on de-identified historic notes; add a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) layer so the LLM cites the patient’s chart snippets inline. These workflows are already live at Epic (via Nuance) and Google Cloud pilots. (grgonline.com, techvify.com, frontiersin.org)

2. “Copilot” Q\&A for Clinicians¶

“Show me this patient’s last EF and all abnormal labs in the past 6 months.”

Build a secure chat interface where the LLM translates natural-language questions into FHIR or SQL queries, pulls structured & unstructured data, and returns a concise answer plus citations to the source note. HIPAA compliance is handled via on-prem deployment or VPC-hosted models with audit logging. (grgonline.com)

3. Coding & Revenue-Cycle Automation¶

  • Auto-suggest ICD-10-CM / CPT codes from the encounter note.
  • Draft prior-authorization letters that quote the payer’s policy language.
  • Summarize “high-utilizer” charts for denial-appeal teams.

LLMs consistently match human coders on precision/recall in published pilots and can shave 1–2 days off claim submission. (travismay.medium.com)

4. Clinical Decision Support & Guideline Guardrails¶

Deploy an agent that cross-checks active orders and meds against clinical guidelines, black-box warnings, and internal best-practice alerts. Because the knowledge base is outside the model, updates propagate instantly without a full re-train. (travismay.medium.com)

5. Population-Health “Ask Me Anything” Layer¶

Let care-management or research teams type plain-English prompts like:

“List diabetic patients age 40-65 with HbA1c > 9 % who haven’t had a retinal exam in 18 months.”

The system uses your 4 M-record corpus as a private knowledge graph, returning CSV cohorts or charts for outreach and trial recruitment.

6. Patient-Engagement Copilots¶

  • Message triage: Auto-draft empathetic portal replies for routine questions.
  • Education bots: Generate medication or procedure FAQs at literacy level of choice. These low-risk, admin-heavy tasks are where many radiology and ambulatory groups have already realized ROI. (businessinsider.com)

7. Synthetic-Data & De-ID Factory¶

Before any fine-tuning, build a pipeline that:

  1. Detects & masks 18 HIPAA identifiers plus free-text PHI.
  2. Generates synthetic records that preserve statistical fidelity for internal analytics or external collaborations.

8. Safety, Audit & Compliance Services¶

  • Red-team the LLM for hallucinations, guideline deviations, or PHI leaks.
  • Produce model cards, bias assessments, and FDA/ONC documentation.
  • Set up continuous prompt-output monitoring with secure human-in-the-loop overrides.

Technical Stack at a Glance¶

  • Model layer: MedLM, GPT-5-Med, or an open-source rival hosted in a VPC enclave.
  • Retrieval: Azure Health Data Services, Elasticsearch-FHIR, or Postgres-pgvector.
  • Guardrails: AWS Bedrock Guardrails or custom policy engine.
  • DevOps: Terraform, Kubernetes, MLflow/W\&B for versioning; SOC 2 logging.

How to Pitch¶

  1. Lead with clinician pain points (after-hours charting, denial rates, burnout).
  2. Bundle quick wins (note summarization) with one “moonshot” (decision support).
  3. Offer a no-PHI, synthetic-data sandbox so legal clears the project quickly.
  4. Show hard ROI—minutes saved per note, coder FTEs reallocated, reduced length of stay.

With 4 million EMRs, your client has the critical mass to train, evaluate, and continuously improve bespoke medical-grade LLM workflows—placing them ahead of most health systems still stuck in pilot purgatory.
